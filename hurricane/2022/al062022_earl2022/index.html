Official Advisory Discussion

At 1100 AM AST (1500 UTC), the center of Hurricane Earl was located near latitude 42.9 North, longitude 53.0 West. Earl is now moving toward the north-northeast near 29 mph (46 km/h). An additional decrease in forward speed is expected today, with Earl moving slowly northeastward to the southeast of Newfoundland tonight through Monday.

Maximum sustained winds have decreased to near 90 mph (150 km/h) with higher gusts. Earl is forecast to become a powerful hurricane-force extratropical low this afternoon. After that, weakening is expected, and Earl’s winds are likely to fall below hurricane force tonight or early Sunday.

Earl is a large cyclone. Hurricane-force winds extend outward up to 90 miles (150 km) from the center and tropical-storm-force winds extend outward up to 415 miles (665 km).

The estimated minimum central pressure is 960 mb (28.35 inches).
